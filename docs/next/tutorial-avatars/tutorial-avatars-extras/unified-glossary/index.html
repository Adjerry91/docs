Eye‚Äã

Eye Gaze‚Äã

The general term that refers to where the eye is looking.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

Generalizations‚Äã

In Unified Expressions, Eye Gaze comes in two subtypes: absolute and expression.

Avatars typically use an armature with the eyes attached to an Eye bone and/or blendshapes to create the overall Eye Gaze.

Absolute vs Expression

When using an armature bone, the direct nature of bone transformation is an absolute Eye Gaze. Using blendshapes is described as an expression as several generalized blendshapes are used to modify the Eye Gaze apparent shape even further.

Shape Explanations‚Äã

In Unified Expressions, Left and Right Eye Gaze expressions direction listed in this way:

Up, Down, Out, and In.

This definition of the direction is very specific and helps contextualize the left and right eye shapes.

This makes it very clear how the shapes function, as Out refers to the shape moving away from the center of the face, while In refers to the shape moving towards the center of the face. Up and Down are already contextually relative to your face as your face is vertically assymetric.

Eye Closed‚Äã

An expression that directly represents how open or closed the eyelid is.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

It is directly tied to VRCFaceTracking Eye Openness, an objective and simple measurement of how open the eyelid is.

Eye Closed is a foundational shape.

Shapes such as Eye Squint and Eye Widen rely on the context of Eye Closed in order to properly be represented as shapes. These other shapes can be refered to as modifier shapes of Eye Closed.

The general term that refers to the pupil dilation of the eye.

Lip Shapes‚Äã

Lip shapes are a subtype of Unified Expressions shape that give many representations of the sophisticated actions that the Orbicularis oris muscle group can do.

Lip Funnel‚Äã

An expression that represents how much the mouth turns into a funnel shape.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

Lip Funnel is part of the Lip shape group.

Lip Funnel is a shape that relies on the context of Jaw Open, Jaw Open, Mouth Upper Up, and Mouth Lower Down. The shape gets modified for each of these shapes.

Lip Suck‚Äã

An expression that represents how much the mouth is trying to tuck into the mouth.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

Lip Suck is part of the Lip shape group.

Lip Suck refers to these different shapes for the left and right face sides respectively:

  • LipSuckUpper
  • LipSuckLower
  • LipSuckCorner

Lip Suck is a shape that relies on the context of Jaw Open, Jaw Open, Mouth Upper Up, and Mouth Lower Down. The shape gets modified for each of these shapes.

Mouth Movements‚Äã

The general term that refers to how the mouth gets moved horizontally.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

All Mouth Movements are part of the Mouth shape group.

The mouth can be moved Left to Right, and is also split up in an Upper and Lower denomination to give the Upper and Lower mouth parts the ability to move horizontally independently from each other.

The following shapes represents these Mouth Movements:

  • MouthUpperRight
  • MouthUpperLeft
  • MouthLowerRight
  • MouthLowerLeft

Mouth Raiser‚Äã

An expression that represents how much the mouth is being lifted by the lower lip pushing upwards.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

Mouth Raiser is part of the Mouth shape group.

The following shapes build up the overall Mouth Raiser:

  • MouthRaiserUpper
  • MouthRaiserLower

The Upper shape is a "corrective" shape that allows the mouth to stay closed when MouthRaiserLower happens. When MouthRaiserUpper is not in use, the MouthRaiserLower shape contextually overlays when this happens.

Mouth Tightener‚Äã

An expression that represents how much the mouth tightens the overall lips together.

Still under construction!

This explanation is still being worked on. Information may change in the future.

Mouth Tightener is part of the Lip shape group.

Mouth Tightener is not part of the Mouth group

Although this expression is labelled as Mouth in the name, the anatomical basis of Mouth Tightener is not based on the other Mouth shapes, but falls under Lip for this reason.

This shape has similarities to Mouth Press, but has a entirely distinct function and shape. The shape that Mouth Tightener represents is the overall lips scrunching together, both horizontally and somewhat vertically. This is disctinct from Mouth Press, which only presses the lips together vertically exclusively and rolls the surrounding mouth in a little bit, similarly to Lip Suck.
