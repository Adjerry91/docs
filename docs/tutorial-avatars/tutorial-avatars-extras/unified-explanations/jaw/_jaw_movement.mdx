### Jaw Movement

***

> The general term that refers to how the 
> jaw gets moved.

***

**Jaw Movement** refers to the cardinal directions 
that the jaw can be positioned, other than **Jaw Open**:

* Jaw Left
* Jaw Right
* Jaw Forward
* Jaw Backward

The jaw can be moved in these such directions.

### Jaw Left

***

### Anatomy

Jaw Left is based on the [medial pterygoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_pterygoid_muscle) and 
[lateral pterygoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_pterygoid_muscle)'s 
general action of shifting the jaw sideways.

### Description

Jaw Left moves the jaw bone leftwards slightly.

### Jaw Right

***

### Anatomy

Jaw Right is based on the [medial pterygoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_pterygoid_muscle) and 
[lateral pterygoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_pterygoid_muscle)'s 
general action of shifting the jaw sideways.

### Description

Jaw Right moves the jaw bone rightwards slightly.

### Jaw Forward

***

### Anatomy

Jaw Forward is based on the 
[lateral pterygoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_pterygoid_muscle)'s 
general action of shifting the jaw forward.

### Description

Jaw Forward moves the jaw bone forwards slightly.

### Jaw Backward

***

### Anatomy

Jaw Backward is based on the [temporalis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temporalis_muscle)
general action of shifting the jaw backwards.

### Description

Jaw Backward moves the jaw bone backwards slightly.
